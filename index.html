Research

My research is centered on harmonic analysis on non-Euclidean geometric structures — Riemannian symmetric spaces, homogeneous trees, and Lie groups — where curvature and algebraic symmetry force a fundamental rethinking of classical Euclidean techniques.

〽
Singular Integrals on Symmetric Spaces
Calderón–Zygmund theory and singular integral operators on Riemannian symmetric spaces and homogeneous trees, where the geometry changes the analytic landscape in essential ways.
⚖
Weighted Estimates for Integral Operators
Muckenhoupt-type weighted inequalities and sharp L^p boundedness for maximal functions and singular integral operators in non-Euclidean settings.
∂
Pseudo-differential Operators on Lie Groups
Symbolic calculus and spectral theory of pseudo-differential operators on compact and non-compact Lie groups, extending the Euclidean microlocal toolkit to curved group-theoretic settings.
∮
Fourier Analysis on Lie Groups
Representation theory, spherical functions, and the Helgason–Fourier transform as tools to study dispersive estimates and spectral multipliers on non-commutative groups.
